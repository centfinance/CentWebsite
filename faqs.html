Frequently asked questions (FAQs) - About Cent wallet

What is Cent?Â 

Cent is a non-custodial decentralized finance (DeFi) wallet, which supports Ethereum, Celo & xDai. It is designed to make life easier for crypto & DeFi users.

What is non-custodial? is it secure?

Noncustodial means that users stay in possession and control of their crypto, this is achieved by storing users' private keys on their device. Unlike traditional wallets, which become a target for hackers due to large amounts of crypto in on one place, Cent removes the target off your back. No one else can access your private keys, not even us!

Does it work on iPhones? what about Android?

Cent is available for download on Google Play and Apple App Store. It works on iPhone, iPad and Android devices.

How can I reach you?

You can reach us at the email provided below. Alternatively, for a quicker response the creators of the Cent wallet can be contacted through Discord. For payments related issues please contact the appropriate business partner to quickly resolve any problems.

Who is it for?

The Cent wallet was created for DeFi users who want to waste less of their crypto on gas and use more of it for DeFi. With support for multiple networks, Cent allows users to access all of their crypto in one place.

Why should I use Cent?

Our philosophy is to make DeFi simple, easy and secure for users. We exist, so you don't have to do the work. We provide automatic cloud back up to ensure private keys are never lost. We've integrated 'Wallet Connect' to allow maximum freedom for DeFi users. Cent also supports multiple networks, ensuring DeFi stays economical.
Wanna see new features? write to us at developer@cent.finance.

What are the payment options on Cent?

Instant bank payments

Debit card payments

Apple Pay
The purchase process shouldn't take more than 5 minutes, although requires basic KYC for the first payment and is available to all EEA countries.

Manual bank transfer
We currently support SEPA and Faster payments manual bank transfers. This method is less convenient and slower than the other methods, but definitely the cheapest one. It is available to all holders of a EUR denominated bank account.These transfers take approximately 24 - 48 hours to complete.

Bank transfers should be sent from an account held in your own name and the transfer should clearly identify you as a sender.

Manual bank payments in the Single Euro Payments Area are settled in sessions -there are 5-6 sessions during the day, but not all banks use all sessions for payment processing. Usually, payments made before 12:00 noon are settled on the same day, while payments sent in the late afternoon or evening will settle on the next working day. Payments made on the weekend will be processed on the next working day or D+2. The speed of payment processing depends on the bank which sends the payment. There are banks which are processing SEPA transfers across border with a value date D+2, i.e. two full working days.
